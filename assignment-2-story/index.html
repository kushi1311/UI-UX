The Nameless Shall Not Rise

The World of swarg and Pataal Lok

The sky above swarg was never dark. The celestial orbs, bound to the great wheels of the heavens, cast their eternal radiance upon the world above, yet their divine touch never graced Pataal Lok, the shadowed realm where the Nameless dwelled, unseen and forgotten.

Veer had grown up beneath this false sky, in the tunnels of Pataal Lok, where the air was thick with the scent of rust and recycled water. The ruling class, the Yamrajas, controlled everything—water, knowledge, even the right to a name. The Nameless were assigned numbers instead.

A great cavern stretched before him, its walls towering like the ribs of some ancient beast. A stone bridge arched gracefully over a still pool, its waters reflecting the dim light that filtered through unseen crevices above. The steps leading down were worn smooth, as if countless feet had once walked this path, though no sign of life remained. Further ahead, the tunnel curved into a hollowed-out chamber, its entrance carved into a perfect spiral, leading downward into deeper darkness.

Veer was Seven-Four-Two, a scavenger from the deep shafts, and he should have lived and died that way. But then he found the Codex of Raktbeej. It was buried beneath the sands of a forgotten chamber, hidden within the depths of Pataal Lok. The place was unlike any other Veer had seen—a vast underground expanse, where nature and time had sculpted a world beneath the surface.

And there, half-buried in the golden sand near the entrance of this cavern, lay the Codex. A stone tablet, covered in intricate carvings, its symbols whispering of a time long before the rule of the Yamrajas. Veer brushed away the dust with trembling fingers, revealing the knowledge that had been stolen from his people.

The texts spoke of a world where the Nameless had ruled, where water was not rationed through biometric locks, and where the Trials—the brutal competition that selected "worthy" Nameless for elevation—were nothing but a lie.

He should have burned it. Instead, carried it along with him. The Trials of Ascendancy were held once every decade. The Nameless believed that the winners would be granted status, wealth, and a place among the Yamrajs. In reality, no one ever saw the winners again. Some whispered that they were executed, others believed they were used in experiments. But the thirst for freedom was stronger than fear.

The Trial grounds were an arena carved into the desert’s heart. Towering monoliths, made of sand-glass and reinforced steel, stretched into the sky, forming a labyrinth designed to test intelligence, endurance, and obedience. The last challenge was always the same—The Offering. A Nameless must prove loyalty by kneeling before the Yamraj High Lords and pledging eternal service. Refusal meant death.
